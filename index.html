Northern Tier Tier List

Why does someplace so cold still have so many more cold things to put in your body? But apart from the crippling root beer addiction we spontaneuosly developed, I have many favorites among our non-consumption based activities:

  1. Downhill Sledding

    2. I can't get hills this good anywhere else, back home either the snow is nonexistant, or the hills have at least two things wrong with them out of height, straightness and steepness. NOTE: The fun part was NOT hauling the gear. May we never use the evil orange sleds ever again.

  2. Cross Country Skiing

    3. The only thing steeper than the learning curve for this were the hills again, but we all improved after a little bit. Unfortunately, we didn't get too far due to all being new, but there were still many downhill sections and tricky sideways walks to make it worthwhile

  3. Ice Fishing

    4. We didn't get to do much of this since the fish refused to come out, but learning about it in the ice shelter was almost as good. The instructor thought my cat plushy was the trip mascot but this was false, any true high adventure mascot has to either be obtained by breaking both Scout and National laws, or piracy (Philmont and Sea base respectively)
